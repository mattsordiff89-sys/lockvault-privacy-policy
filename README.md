# Sordifense – App Lock Privacy Policy

**Effective date: July 17, 2026**

Sordifense – App Lock, formerly known as LockVault, is an Android application developed by Sordiff Studios that allows users to protect selected apps using their device’s fingerprint, face recognition, PIN, pattern, or password.

This Privacy Policy explains what information Sordifense – App Lock accesses, how that information is used, and how it is protected.

## Information Sordifense – App Lock Accesses

### Installed app information

Sordifense – App Lock accesses the names and package identifiers of apps that can be launched on the device. This allows the user to choose which apps Sordifense – App Lock should protect.

The selected-app list is stored locally on the user’s device.

### Accessibility Service

Sordifense – App Lock uses the Android AccessibilityService API to detect when an app selected by the user is opened or when the active app changes.

This access is used only to:

- Detect when a protected app is opened.
- Display the Sordifense – App Lock authentication screen.
- Relock protected apps after the user leaves them.
- Help protect information when the Recent Apps screen is opened.

Sordifense – App Lock does not use Accessibility access to:

- Read messages or conversations.
- Read passwords or typed text.
- Read photos or files.
- Record the screen.
- Press buttons or perform actions on the user’s behalf.
- Collect the contents displayed inside other apps.

Sordifense – App Lock’s Accessibility Service is configured not to retrieve window contents.

Users must enable Sordifense – App Lock App Protection manually in Android Accessibility settings and may disable it at any time.

## Biometric and Device Authentication

Sordifense – App Lock uses Android’s system authentication interface.

Fingerprint, face recognition, PIN, pattern, and password verification are handled by the Android operating system. Sordifense – App Lock does not receive, collect, store, or transmit biometric information or the user’s device password.

Sordifense – App Lock only receives confirmation from Android indicating whether authentication succeeded or failed.

## Data Collection

Sordifense – App Lock does not:

- Require an account.
- Directly collect names, addresses, phone numbers, or full payment-card or banking information.
- Collect messages, photos, passwords, or contacts.
- Collect precise location information.
- Use advertising or analytics services.
- Sell or share user information.
- Upload the selected-app list or protection settings to a server.

Sordifense – App Lock’s settings are stored locally on the device.

## In-App Purchases

Sordifense – App Lock may offer a one-time Pro upgrade through Google Play.

Payments are processed by Google Play. Sordifense – App Lock does not directly receive, collect, or store the user’s full payment-card or banking information.

## Data Sharing

Sordifense – App Lock does not sell, rent, trade, or share user information with third parties.

## Data Storage and Deletion

Selected-app information, protection settings, and disclosure preferences are stored locally on the user’s device.

Users can delete this information by:

- Removing selected apps inside Sordifense – App Lock.
- Clearing Sordifense – App Lock’s storage through Android Settings.
- Uninstalling Sordifense – App Lock.

## Security

Sordifense – App Lock uses Android’s system security and authentication features to help protect access to the application and selected apps.

No security system can guarantee complete protection. Users should continue using a secure device screen lock and keep their Android software updated.

## Children’s Privacy

Sordifense – App Lock does not knowingly collect personal information from children or any other users.

## Changes to This Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated if Sordifense – App Lock’s features or data practices change. Updates will be posted on this page with a revised effective date.
